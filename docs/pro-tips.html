Pro Tips

Audience: Experienced players consistently clearing the Spire who want to push Ascension and tighten their decision-making. Assumes fluency with all mechanics.

Think in breakpoints, not totals

  • What matters is whether you survive the intent and whether you kill on schedule. Count exact damage: enough block to negate the hit, enough damage to hit the kill turn.
  • Sequence debuffs for thresholds: Vulnerable before your burst; Weak on the turn an enemy makes its big swing.
  • Plan the whole turn before playing a card — card order changes draw, energy, and scaling-power triggers.

Scaling vs. front-load

  • Long fights (bosses, big elites) reward scaling: Strength/Focus, Powers, Poison, orb engines. Short fights reward front-load: cheap burst and AoE.
  • Most losing decks are all front-load and no scaling, or a scaling engine with no early survival. Draft so you cover both by Act 2.
  • Value card draw and energy highly — an engine that plays your whole deck makes every other card better.

Playing the Ascension modifiers (0–20)

Difficulty is cumulative. Adjust to the level you actually selected:

  • A2–A4 / A17–A19 (enemies hit harder & gain Strength): in this version higher Ascension grants enemies extra Strength, so multi-hit enemies scale viciously — prioritise Weak and killing the multi-attackers first.
  • A7–A9 (enemies have more HP): your "kill turn" slips later; lean harder into scaling and AoE, and don't rely on exact-lethal math from low Ascension.
  • A6 / A14 (start lower / less Max HP): early elites are deadlier; route around risky early fights until your deck comes online.
  • A11 (one fewer potion slot): potions are scarcer — save them for boss breakpoints rather than convenience.
  • A10 (Ascender's Bane curse): an unremovable dead card; account for the extra deck dilution when you decide how aggressively to thin.
  • A13 (bosses give 25% less gold): tighten shop priorities; removal first.
  • A20 (second Act-3 boss): you must beat two Act-3 bosses back-to-back — keep enough sustain/Block to survive the encore without a rest in between.

Archetype execution

  • Silent — Poison: Once stacked, Poison ignores Block and scales multiplicatively with effects that double it. Catalyst-style multipliers convert a small stack into a kill — set up, then pop.
  • Silent — Shiv/Discard: Treat discard as a resource; cards that reward discarding turn "wasted" draws into tempo and energy.
  • Ironclad — Strength: Strength multiplies with multi-hit and per-hit bonuses; a single big buff plus repeated attacks snowballs. Protect the engine with Block and heal.
  • Ironclad — Exhaust: Cards that pay off when you Exhaust turn dead/curse cards into block, draw, and damage; curate what you exhaust and when.
  • Defect — Frost/Focus: Frost block scales with Focus and never expires while channelled; a Focus base makes a wall that also wins long fights.
  • Defect — Lightning/Orbs: Slot count + Focus + Evoke timing is the whole game; line up Evokes for the turn an enemy is most vulnerable.

Discipline that separates good from great

  • Skip more rewards. At high Ascension the average card makes your deck worse.
  • Pre-commit to a plan by the end of Act 1. Reactive drafting produces incoherent decks.
  • Respect unknown intents and "?" events — at high Ascension the downside swings are what end runs, not the average outcome.
  • Bank potions for the fights that actually threaten lethality.
